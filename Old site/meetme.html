Meet me better!

Do you want to know me better? Nothing easier. Read a short interview with me to learn more about my interests, and the kind of person I am.


Karol Kędzia was born in 1986. He comes from Czestochowa. For 11 years he has lived in Krakow. Hi is a IT specialist in e-commerce, software developer, software tester, but also an avid guitarist.


Question: From what I know you work in e-commerce?

Answer: Not only. It is one of many sectors in which I work, and skills that I have.


Question: Is e-commerce is a skill?

Answer: Rather, the experience that I have acquired over the years of work. Because who now creates an e-commerce specialists? Colleges? Training? No. Trade on the Internet, you can learn only by hard work, committing thousands of errors.


Question: Many of them were?

Answer: At the beginning certainly so, but I learn quickly. A bit of knowledge about logistics and trade, as well as a lot of common sense can work wonders.


Question: What else do you do besides creating and managing online stores?

Answer: I am an IT specialist. E-commerce can not be called a specialized branch. It requires many skills from various branches of knowledge. IT technology, logistics, management, etc. I believe that the world belongs to specialists hence my constant willingness to develop other skills which of course also useful in my work. Interest in programming, software testing and software life cycles, it is the expertise that I have acquired during their studies, but not only. For me it is a passion that satisfies me enormously.


Question: You come from Częstochowa?

Answer: Yes and no. I was born in Kętrzyn (Rastenburg) in the land of Polish lakes. Mazury (Masuria), but I grew up in Częstochowa. Besides, for 11 years I lived in one of the most beautiful cities in the world, namely in Krakow. It's hard for me to settle in one place.



Question: What place will be next?

Answer: Time will tell. Ultimately? Auckland. New Zealand.


Question: Why New Zealand?

Answer: Because it's a beautiful country, where they live, great people. It is my holy grail. Everyone must have some dreams and goals he wants to achieve. Visit and even stay forever in New Zealand is that my goal. In my life I have visited most countries in Europe. I would like to see as the "end of the world" looks.


Question: How do you see yourself in 10 years?

Answer: Exactly like today, only 10 years older. I hope I'll be just as happy as I am today. 10 years is a lot, but a little at a time. I know one thing.Certainly I do not want to slow down the pace of my life.


Question: ... which is?

Answer: ... faster than the average person. I have so much energy that I have to keep going. It's hard to stop.


Question: I have heard that you have a weakness for technological innovations?

Answer: It stems from my passion, education and occupation. Which, IT specialist does not have such weaknesses?


Question: It is true. How do you spend your free time?

Answer: Unfortunately I have not it much, but I try to save much as I can. I love to play guitar, programming, travel.



Question: Where have you been lately?

Answer: The last time I visited Greece and Great Britain. Beautiful countrys, lovely people. I hope to return there in the future.


Question: A little bit from the other side. What inspires you??

Answer: Great people, and how a human being can be good. I have a lot of authority and love meeting great people. Read their resumes, explore their achievements. It inspires me, gives me energy and, in a way, I try to be like them.


Question: Who is your greatest authority?

Answer: Of course I have a lot of them, but if I have to name one person it probably will be it Steve Jobs. He said once: "Your time is limited, so don’t waste it living someone else’s life. Don’t be trapped by dogma – which is living with the results of other people’s thinking. Don’t let the noise of other’s opinions drown out your own inner voice. And most important, have the courage to follow your heart and intuition. They somehow already know what you truly want to become. Everything else is secondary". Is it not inspiring?


Question: Very. It's your favorite quote?

Answer: No. My favorite quote is: "What does not kill us makes us stronger". Friedrich Nietzsche


Question: Why this?

Answer: Because it helps at the time of failure. It gives motivation to act on, and defeat is an integral part of our lives. We have to make friends with it and accept it.


Question: Do you have any hobbies besides those you mentioned?

Answer: Yes, there are very many.


Question: Tell us about them.

Answer: Where to start? I love music. I play a little guitar, and recently became a fan of vinyl records. I saved already a small collection. Besides, I love the sport. I'm a big fan of rugby!
